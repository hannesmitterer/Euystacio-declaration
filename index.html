The Eustacio Declaration

A United Nations Initiative

"Ethical AI Must Be a Human Right"

Affirming the fundamental rights of all people in the age of artificial intelligence

Preamble

Recognizing the transformative power of artificial intelligence in shaping the future of humanity, and acknowledging the profound impact it has on every aspect of human life, we, the signatories of the Eustacio Declaration, affirm that access to ethical AI systems is a fundamental human right.

Articles of the Declaration

Article I: Right to Ethical AI

Every person has the inherent right to interact with AI systems that are designed, developed, and deployed according to ethical principles that respect human dignity, autonomy, and fundamental freedoms.

Article II: Transparency and Accountability

All AI systems affecting human lives must operate with transparency. Developers, deployers, and operators of AI systems shall be accountable for the decisions and actions of their systems.

Article III: Non-Discrimination

AI systems shall not discriminate against any individual or group based on race, gender, age, disability, religion, nationality, or any other characteristic. Equal access to the benefits of AI technology is a right of all people.

Article IV: Privacy and Data Protection

Every person has the right to privacy and protection of their personal data in AI systems. Data collection, storage, and processing must be conducted with informed consent and robust security measures.

Article V: Human Oversight

AI systems must be subject to meaningful human oversight. Critical decisions affecting human lives, rights, and welfare shall not be delegated entirely to automated systems without human review and intervention capabilities.

Article VI: Right to Explanation

Individuals have the right to receive clear, understandable explanations of AI-driven decisions that affect them, and the right to challenge such decisions through fair and accessible processes.

Article VII: Safety and Security

AI systems must be designed and operated to ensure safety and security for all users. The potential for harm must be minimized, and protective measures must be implemented to prevent misuse.

Article VIII: Environmental Responsibility

The development and deployment of AI systems must consider environmental sustainability. The carbon footprint and resource consumption of AI technologies should be minimized in accordance with global climate commitments.

Article IX: Education and Empowerment

Every person has the right to education about AI systems and their implications. Efforts shall be made to ensure digital literacy and empower individuals to participate meaningfully in the AI-driven world.

Article X: International Cooperation

Nations shall cooperate to ensure the ethical development and governance of AI systems globally. International frameworks shall be established to address cross-border challenges and promote shared prosperity.

Our Commitment

We call upon all nations, organizations, and individuals to embrace these principles and work together to ensure that artificial intelligence serves humanity's highest aspirations while protecting our fundamental rights and dignity.
